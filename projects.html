Still Life in Digital

2015 — Present. In collaboration with Marisa Parham: essays, maps, and interactive visualizations on Black life, trauma, and social media. I wrote about some of my preliminary work with the Twitter API on my blog here.

The Recirculator

2015 — Present. A web app that lets you randomly check out books that have never before circulated within the Five College library system, and learn more about the collection through data visualizations.

E.LIT / NET.ART

2013 — 2016. An introduction to and exploration of electronic literature and Internet art, comprising pop-up IRL and URL galleries, workshops, and a screening program. Collected under the elit.jeffreymoro.com imprimatur.

The_Critical_Is

2014 — 2015. A project I co-edited exploring new ways of teaching, writing, and thinking about video games and interactive narratives. My collaborators and I presented a poster at HASTAC 2015; I shared the essay "Strange Creatures Made of Memory," on glitch aesthetics and new possibilities for play. Funded by the Association for Computers and the Humanities.

AIRLab

2014 — 2015. In collaboration with Jon Caris and Eric Poehler, a hybrid makerspace and workgroup exploring, historicizing, and critiquing creative robotics in the undergraduate classroom and the world at large. Funded by Five College Digital Humanities.

Shakespeare Film and the Media of Translation

2013. This is a longer piece that examines subtitles, intertitles, and other “media of translation” in a wide range of English and non-English Shakespeare film, and argues for renewed attention to the technologies that we use to translate media. It was my senior thesis in English, for which I received magna cum laude.

Media Archaeology, Climate Change, and the Digital (Non)humanities

January 2016. A week-long course exploring the media history, materiality, and infrastructures of digital and network technologies, and interrogating how the digital humanities and media studies might theorize and respond practically to climate upheaval and the idea of the "apocalypse" more generally.

The New Rigor

2015. In collaboration with Five College Digital Humanities: A series of one-day conferences with the aim of developing a community sense in academia of how to best support and engage digital scholarship across faculty, staff, students, and other constituencies.

E.LIT / NET.ART: An Extraordinarily Brief Survey of Digital Literature, 1975 – 2015

January 2015. A week-long survey of electronic literature and Internet art, focusing on questions of form, genre, and materiality. Part of my larger E.LIT / NET.ART curatorial project.

Lightning Talk: Plastic, Logic, & Time

2016. A lightning talk that I gave at Five College Digital Humanities’ 2016 Community Showcase, about Super Mario World, Flappy Bird, and the creative and evocative stakes of media archaeological practice.

Lightning Talk: The Space of Mexico City

2014. A lightning talk that I gave at Five College Digital Humanities’ 2014 Kickoff Celebration, about “The Space of Mexico City,” a project I assist on that’s building an interactive, experiential map of Mexico City’s lived environment.

Talk: Undergraduate Digital Humanities, Three Ways

2014. I was invited to speak at a NERCOMP (Northeast Regional Computing Program) conference called “Emerging Digital Scholars: Undergraduates and Digital Humanities” on strategies Five College Digital Humanities uses to encourage student research and engagement with the digital humanities.

Video: Introduction to Twine

2015. A tutorial video I made with Kimberly Bain on how to get started using Twine to make rudimentary text adventure games.

Video + Prezi: Five College Digital Humanities

2014. This is a video and interactive presentation that I designed and scripted in Prezi, exploring Five College Digital Humanities’ programs.

detritus.website

2014 — Present. An ongoing investigation into what emerges when I indulge my worst ideas.

No Wind Blows Against the Pines

2012 — 2013. I wrote and directed No Wind Blows Against the Pines, adapted from two Noh plays, Pining Wind and The Sumida River. See photos here, credit to Julie Keresztes. Low-res performance video is on Vimeo. It was my undergrad thesis in Theater & Dance, for which I received magna cum laude.

Two Winds

2012. A dance-theater piece I choreographed in collaboration with Skye Landgraf and Morgan Brown. It was a kind of first draft for No Wind Blows. See photos here, credit to Risalat Khan.

Shakespeare on the Quad Theater Company

2011 — 2013. I co-founded, produced, and occasionally acted for Shakespeare on the Quad Theater Company. We mounted free outdoor performances of Shakespeare comedies for the Pioneer Valley community.
